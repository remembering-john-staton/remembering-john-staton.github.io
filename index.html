The Artist
John
Staton

John Staton was a Maryland-based artist, cartoonist, and worldbuilder whose work spanned independent comics, fan art, and original IP across more than two decades. His DeviantArt gallery — archived here in full — documents a creative life that was restlessly inventive, deeply funny, and fuelled by an encyclopaedic love of anime, classic Trek, and action animation.

His flagship creation, DMV, imagined a cyberpunk dystopia in which a 6x3 metre rectangle of road surface had become the most contested real estate on earth. Its heroine, America Jones — combat-trained meter maid — is one of the most distinctive original characters in indie comics. John worked the concept through comic strips, pin-ups, and worldbuilding notes across many years, aiming to have a full comic ready for Otakon.

Alongside DMV he developed The Streamers, an ensemble of military-branch magical girls each representing a branch of the U.S. armed forces. He contributed guest art to Angry Viking Press's Evil Diva and Adam Warren's Empowered Special #4: Animal Style, and produced an exclusive variant cover for Jeremy Whitley's Princeless: The Pirate Princess #1 for Big Planet Comics.

His Inktober 2016 run — 31 consecutive chibi Star Trek ships — remains one of the most committed fan art series in the gallery. His opinions on Enterprise were strongly held and entirely correct.
