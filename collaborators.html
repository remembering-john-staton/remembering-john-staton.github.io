Working Artists & Collaborators
Adam Warren@adamwarren99Published Collaborator

John contributed to Warren's Empowered Special #4: Animal Style — the five Animal Style Designs pieces in this archive were produced for that book. Warren is one of the artists John cited most as a direct influence, particularly for the Dirty Pair / Lovely Angels work. John once described him as drawing those characters "with equal shamelessness" and clearly worshipped the line work.

Jeremy Whitley@princelessPublished Collaborator

John produced the exclusive variant cover for Princeless: The Pirate Princess #1 for Big Planet Comics, January 2015. He colored Adrienne Ashe first — "she is the star after all, supporting cast has to wait their turn." One of the most finished pieces in the archive.

Fred Perry@fredgdperryFan Art Subject · Correspondent

Creator of Gold Digger — the most extensive, long-running self-contained North American comic produced by a single creator, running 300 issues from 1991 to 2022. Perry wrote, drew, inked, colored, and lettered it himself on a monthly schedule for thirty years. A Marine Corps veteran who conceived the idea during the Gulf War ("There wasn't anything to stop me from drawing pin-ups for my crew"), and describes the series as Indiana Jones meets Final Fantasy. John drew Gold Digger fan art continuously across the archive, corresponded with Perry in comments, and used his account as a reaction stamp. Perry told John once: "What is this, some weird sort of cry for help?" John included that in his own artist description.

PM
Phil Moyeraka Philip-M, SD-Phil, AerianneColorist · Friend · Creative Partner

One of John's closest friends and creative collaborators. Also known online as Aerianne — his own original character, Aerianne Conner, a fighter pilot and Highlander-style immortal and commander of the Terrapin Corps, an elite intergalactic squadron. John drew Aerianne for Phil multiple times in the archive. Phil colored Uzi Cat, Tachiku, Pool Girls, and others in what John described as a mutual gift exchange. He's a University of Maryland graduate — hence the Terrapin name — and has been drawing and archiving artwork since 1994.

Bandeau@bandeauDesigner · Top Commenter

A professional graphic designer and illustrator from British Columbia who went freelance after nearly a decade in design — and the single most present person in John's entire archive. 185 comments across 13 pieces, plus credited directly with influencing costume design: John noted AJ's pants in Maid and Machine were "suggested by @bandeau so I thought I'd try them for a spin." That level of collaboration, running across years.

Angry Viking Press@angry-viking-pressPublisher

The indie studio behind Evil Diva — the comic about a demon girl who wanted to be an angel. John contributed guest art and drew the Snow Devil pencils for a project with them. He used their account as a reaction stamp, which says something about how he felt about the book.
